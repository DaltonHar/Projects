How did I get here?

I was never a fan of college right out of high school. I went to a local community college on and off for 2 years (completing two semesters) and finally realizing that I just couldn't do it, at least not right after highschool. I decided to try Medical Coding after working at restaurants, mowing yards, and working at a daycare. To get certified to be a medical coder I needed to receive a CPC (certified professional coder) which I was able to do after completing an 8-month long online course. Once I received the certification, I began looking for a job that would help me get my foot in the door. Unluckily, I didn't find any, so I applied and was accepted to work at Spectrum. I have always been a good "people-person" and have enjoyed troubleshooting and/or solving problems, so I decided to try it. While working as an Internet Repair agent at Spectrum, I have still been interested in coding itself. That brings me to where I am now, but I will go into more detail as to why I began coding here.
